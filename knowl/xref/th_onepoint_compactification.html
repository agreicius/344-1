Theorem 1.17.5. One-point compactification.

Let \((X,\mathcal{T})\) be a topological space, and let \((X^*,\mathcal{T}^*)\) the Alexandroff extension of \(X\) as defined in Topological specimenÂ 16.
  1. \(\mathcal{T}^*\) is a topology, and the inclusion map \(i\colon X\rightarrow X^*\) is an open immersion with respect to this topology.
  2. \(X^*\) is compact.
  3. The following statements are equivalent.
    1. \(\{p_\infty\}\) is open in \(X^*\text{.}\)
    2. \(X\) is not dense in \(X^*\)
    3. \(X\) is compact.
  4. The following statements are equivalent.
    1. \(X^*\) is Hausdorff.
    2. \(X\) is locally compact Hausdorff and not compact.
